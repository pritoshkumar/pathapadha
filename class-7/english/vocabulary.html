Vocabulary Building

📚 Prefixes

A prefix is added to the beginning of a word to change its meaning.

  • un- = not → unhappy, unable, unfair
  • re- = again → rewrite, rebuild, replay
  • dis- = opposite → disagree, disappear, dislike
  • mis- = wrongly → misunderstand, misspell, mislead
  • pre- = before → preview, predict, prepare
  • im-/in- = not → impossible, invisible, incorrect

Suffixes

A suffix is added to the end of a word to change its meaning or part of speech.

  • -ful = full of → beautiful, hopeful, careful
  • -less = without → careless, hopeless, fearless
  • -tion/-sion = makes a noun → education, decision, creation
  • -ly = makes an adverb → quickly, happily, slowly
  • -ness = makes a noun → kindness, darkness, happiness
  • -able/-ible = can be → readable, visible, possible

Synonyms and Antonyms

Synonyms are words with similar meanings. Antonyms are words with opposite meanings.

Synonyms: big = large, happy = glad, fast = quick
Antonyms: hot ↔ cold, big ↔ small, happy ↔ sad

Tip: Learning synonyms helps you avoid repeating the same words in your writing!
