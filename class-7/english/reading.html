Reading Comprehension Skills

📖 Reading Prose Passages

When reading a prose passage, follow these steps:

  1. Skim — Read quickly to get the main idea
  2. Scan — Look for specific information or keywords
  3. Read carefully — Understand every sentence in detail
  4. Summarize — Write the main points in your own words
Tip: Always read the questions first before reading the passage. This helps you know what to look for!

Poetry Appreciation

Poems use special techniques to create meaning and beauty:

  • Rhyme — Words that sound alike at the end (cat/hat)
  • Rhythm — The beat or pattern of stressed syllables
  • Imagery — Words that create pictures in your mind
  • Simile — Comparing using "like" or "as" (brave as a lion)
  • Metaphor — Direct comparison (the world is a stage)
  • Personification — Giving human qualities to non-human things

Unseen Passages

Unseen passages test your ability to understand text you have never read before. Key strategies:

  • Read the passage at least twice
  • Underline key words and phrases
  • Look for the topic sentence (usually the first sentence of each paragraph)
  • Answer in complete sentences using your own words
  • Give evidence from the passage to support your answer
