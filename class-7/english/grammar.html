Grammar Concepts

📝 Clauses and Phrases

A clause has a subject and a verb. A phrase does not have both.

Types of Clauses

  • Independent clause — Can stand alone as a sentence. Example: "She reads books."
  • Dependent clause — Cannot stand alone. Example: "Because she loves reading..."
  • Relative clause — Gives more information using who/which/that. Example: "The boy who won the race is my friend."
Examples:
"I will go home when the bell rings." (dependent clause)
"The cat that sits on the wall is mine." (relative clause)

Reported Speech (Indirect Speech)

When we report what someone said without using their exact words:

  • Direct: He said, "I am hungry."
  • Indirect: He said that he was hungry.

Key Changes

  • Present tense becomes past tense
  • "I" changes to "he/she" (matching the speaker)
  • "Today" becomes "that day", "tomorrow" becomes "the next day"
  • "Here" becomes "there", "this" becomes "that"
Direct: She said, "I will come tomorrow."
Indirect: She said that she would come the next day.

Conditionals

Conditional sentences describe situations that depend on a condition being met.

  • Zero conditional (facts): "If you heat water, it boils."
  • First conditional (likely future): "If it rains, I will stay home."
  • Second conditional (unlikely/imaginary): "If I were a bird, I would fly."
Pattern: If + condition, result
"If you study hard, you will pass the exam." (First conditional)
