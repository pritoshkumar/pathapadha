Tenses — Past, Present & Future

📘 Marigold Part 3 (NCERT)

Tenses tell us WHEN something happens. Learn the three main tenses: Past (already happened), Present (happening now), and Future (will happen).

🕐 Present Tense — Happening Now
The present tense tells us what is happening RIGHT NOW or what happens regularly. ✅ I eat rice every day. ✅ She plays in the garden. ✅ The birds sing in the morning. ✅ We go to school at 8 o'clock. 📌 Signal words: now, every day, always, usually, today
⏪ Past Tense — Already Happened
The past tense tells us what ALREADY happened — yesterday, last week, or long ago. ✅ I ate rice yesterday. ✅ She played in the garden. ✅ The birds sang in the morning. ✅ We went to school early. 📌 Signal words: yesterday, last week, ago, once upon a time 📌 Many past tense verbs end in -ed: walked, played, jumped, cooked
⏩ Future Tense — Will Happen
The future tense tells us what WILL happen later — tomorrow, next week, or in the future. ✅ I will eat rice tomorrow. ✅ She will play in the garden. ✅ The birds will sing in the morning. ✅ We will go to school early. 📌 Signal words: tomorrow, next week, soon, later 📌 Use "will" before the verb: will eat, will play, will go
