Vocabulary

Prefixes, Suffixes & Compound Words

📘 Marigold Part 3 (NCERT)

Build your word power! Learn how prefixes and suffixes change word meanings, and discover how two small words can join to make compound words.

🔧 Prefixes — Added Before a Word
A prefix is a group of letters added BEFORE a word to change its meaning. un + happy = unhappy (not happy) re + write = rewrite (write again) dis + like = dislike (not like) pre + heat = preheat (heat before)
un-not / opposite
re-again
dis-not / opposite
pre-before
mis-wrongly
🔩 Suffixes — Added After a Word
A suffix is a group of letters added AFTER a word to change its meaning. play + ful = playful (full of play) kind + ness = kindness (state of being kind) teach + er = teacher (one who teaches) hope + less = hopeless (without hope)
-fulfull of
-lesswithout
-nessstate of being
-erone who does
-lyin a way
🔗 Compound Words — Two Words Joined
A compound word is made by joining two smaller words together. sun + flower = sunflower 🌻 rain + bow = rainbow 🌈 book + shelf = bookshelf 📚 foot + ball = football ⚽ water + fall = waterfall 💧 bed + room = bedroom 🛏️ star + fish = starfish ⭐ tooth + brush = toothbrush 🪥
📌 Quick Reference
Remember: 📍 Prefix = BEFORE the word (un-, re-, dis-, pre-, mis-) 📍 Suffix = AFTER the word (-ful, -less, -ness, -er, -ly) 📍 Compound = TWO words joined (sunflower, rainbow) Tip: Look for these patterns when you read new words!
