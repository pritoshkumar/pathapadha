Adjectives — Describing Words
🎨
CONCEPT
What is an Adjective?
A word that describes a noun
An adjective tells us more about a noun. It describes the size, colour, shape, or quality of something. Adjectives answer: What kind? How many? Which one?
💡 The tall tree has green leaves.
She wore a beautiful dress.
There are five mangoes on the table.
📏
CONCEPT
Degrees of Comparison
Positive, Comparative, Superlative
Positive: tall, big, fast (describing one thing) Comparative: taller, bigger, faster (comparing two things) Superlative: tallest, biggest, fastest (comparing three or more)
💡 Ravi is tall. Mohan is taller than Ravi. Suresh is the tallest boy in the class.
Adverbs — How, When, Where
🏃
CONCEPT
What is an Adverb?
A word that describes a verb
An adverb tells us how, when, or where an action happens. How? — slowly, quickly, carefully When? — today, yesterday, soon Where? — here, there, everywhere
💡 She ran quickly. (How?)
We will go tomorrow. (When?)
The cat sat there. (Where?)
Prepositions & Conjunctions
📍
CONCEPT
Prepositions
Words that show position or time
Prepositions show the relationship between a noun and other words in a sentence. Common prepositions: in, on, at, under, behind, between, near, above, below, beside
💡 The book is on the table.
The cat is under the bed.
She arrived at 5 o'clock.
🔗
CONCEPT
Conjunctions
Words that join sentences
Conjunctions are joining words. They connect words, phrases, or sentences. Common conjunctions: and, but, or, so, because, although, if, when
💡 I like mangoes and bananas.
She was tired but she kept working.
We stayed inside because it was raining.
