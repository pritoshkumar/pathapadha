Homophones — Same Sound, Different Meaning
👂
CONCEPT
What are Homophones?
Words that sound the same but have different spellings and meanings
Homophones are words that sound the same when you say them, but they are spelled differently and mean different things.

Homonyms — Same Word, Different Meaning
🔄
CONCEPT
What are Homonyms?
Words that are spelled the same but have different meanings
Homonyms are words with the same spelling but different meanings depending on how they are used in a sentence.
💡 bat — an animal that flies at night / a stick used in cricket
bank — where we keep money / the side of a river
ring — jewellery for your finger / the sound a bell makes
bark — the sound a dog makes / the outer layer of a tree
light — not heavy / brightness from the sun
Root Words — Build New Words
🌱
CONCEPT
Prefixes and Suffixes
Adding parts to the beginning or end of a word
A prefix is added to the beginning of a word. A suffix is added to the end of a word. The root word is the base word without any additions. Common prefixes: un- (not), re- (again), dis- (not), pre- (before) Common suffixes: -ful (full of), -less (without), -ly (in a way), -ness (state of)
💡 un + happy = unhappy (not happy)
re + write = rewrite (write again)
care + ful = careful (full of care)
hope + less = hopeless (without hope)
slow + ly = slowly (in a slow way)
